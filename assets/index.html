MARCH 2024 - APRIL 2024

Analyzing S&P 500 Trends and Market Efficiency: A Time Series Modeling Approach

In the project titled "Analyzing S&P 500 Trends and Market Efficiency: A Time Series Modeling Approach," I conducted an extensive analysis of S&P 500 price data spanning from 2007 to 2023. This involved visualizing price and return data, conducting statistical tests including t-tests, Arch and Box tests, fitting three GARCH models (m1, m2, m3), and performing a variance ratio test to assess market efficiency. Additionally, analyses were conducted for holding periods of 2, 5, and 10 days. The project provided insights into S&P 500 trends, the statistical properties of returns, and implications for market efficiency, while also acknowledging limitations in making blanket assumptions about market efficiency based solely on index data.

FEB 2024 - MARCH 2024

Factor Analysis for Investment Strategies

In this project, the focus was on analyzing different financial factors to determine their effectiveness in predicting future returns for investment strategies. The factors analyzed included Operating Margin, Price to Book ratio, Return on Equity (ROE), and Rho. After collecting and cleaning the data, various transformations were applied to standardize and prepare the factors for analysis. Using Information Coefficient (IC) values as a metric, the effectiveness of each factor in predicting future returns was evaluated. The results indicated that the Price to Book ratio exhibited the highest IC value, suggesting it as the most effective predictor of future returns among the factors analyzed. This project highlights the importance of factor analysis in identifying valuable insights for investment decision-making.
